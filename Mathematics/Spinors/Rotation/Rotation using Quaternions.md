## Rotation in $SU(2)$

An Matrix $U \in SU(2)$ can be represented as [[Quaternion]]:

$$U = \begin{bmatrix}
a + bi & -c+di \\
c+di & a-bi
\end{bmatrix}$$
$$q = a + bi + cj + dk$$
where the Hypersphere $S^3$ is given by
$$a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + d^2 = 1$$

> [!Note]
> Rotations are represented as Quaternions, where computational performance is an issue.
